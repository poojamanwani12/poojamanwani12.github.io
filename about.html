About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher at the Centre for Climate Studies, IIT Bombay, focusing on aerosol chemistry, source apportionment, and air-quality management. My research bridges environmental science and data analytics — integrating receptor modelling (EPA-PMF 5.0), machine learning, and uncertainty quantification to characterise PM2.5 sources and health-relevant chemical metrics across Indian regions.

My current work explores aerosol acidity, oxidative potential, and regional variability to assess atmospheric reactivity and exposure risks. I also design interactive dashboards in Power BI to visualise and communicate research outcomes for policy and public awareness.

Beyond research, I enjoy organising technical workshops, mentoring students, and pursuing creative outlets like journaling, aerial acrobat yoga, and calligraphy.
