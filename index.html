About

I am a Ph.D. researcher at the Centre for Climate Studies, IIT Bombay (CGPA 8.2; expected Dec 2025). My work brings together atmospheric chemistry, receptor modelling, and data science to generate actionable evidence for air-quality management.

Highlights

  • PMF–ML hybrid modelling for source attribution
  • Aerosol acidity, neutralisation & oxidative potential
  • Meta‑analysis & dashboarding for Indian source apportionment

Research

Focus areas

  • PM2.5 chemistry & events — biomass burning, dust intrusions, festive fireworks and their regional impacts.
  • Meteorology & transport — seasonal drivers, dispersion and potential source regions via back‑trajectory clusters.
  • Aerosol acidity & health — multi‑proxy acidity and oxidative potential, regional comparisons.
  • Source apportionment & ML — EPA‑PMF 5.0 + ML to isolate meteorology‑driven variability.
  • Uncertainty & sensitivity — fuzzy logic + Monte Carlo for health‑risk assessment of PM2.5.
