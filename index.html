Pooja Manwani

Atmospheric scientist focused on aerosols & air quality. Ph.D. scholar at IIT Bombay working on PM2.5 chemistry, source apportionment (PMF+ML), aerosol acidity and uncertainty for health-relevant insights.

✉️ Email 🧬 ORCID 🎓 Google Scholar 💼 LinkedIn

Research Interests

  • Aerosol chemistry & PM2.5 events — biomass burning, dust intrusions, festive fireworks and their regional air-quality impacts.
  • Source apportionment & ML — EPA‑PMF 5.0 + hybrid ML to decouple meteorology-driven variability.
  • Aerosol acidity & health metrics — multi‑proxy acidity, neutralisation, oxidative potential; regional comparisons.
  • Uncertainty quantification — fuzzy logic and Monte Carlo analysis for health‑risk assessment.

Highlights

  • NCAP–COALESCE WP‑2 Student Coordinator for 11 field sites—sampling, QA/QC and data management.
  • Interactive Power BI dashboard for a pan‑India source apportionment meta‑analysis.
  • Conference papers at EGU and AGU on PM2.5 source‑meteorology nexus and aerosol acidity.
